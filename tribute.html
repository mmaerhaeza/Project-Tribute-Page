José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

Also known as Dr. Jose Rizal

A Filipino nationalist, writer and polymath active at the end of the Spanish colonial period of the Philippines. He is popularly considered a national hero.

Dr. Jose Rizal's Timeline:

Early Life and Education

  • June 19, 1861: Born in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines.
  • 1869: Learned the alphabet and basic reading from his mother, Teodora Alonso.
  • 1870: Studied in Biñan, Laguna, under Justiniano Aquino Cruz.
  • 1872: Entered Ateneo Municipal de Manila as a boarding student.
  • 1876: Received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Ateneo, graduating with highest honors.
  • 1877: Enrolled at the University of Santo Tomas (UST) to study philosophy and letters.

Travels and Writings (Europe)

  • 1882: Left for Spain, secretly, to continue his studies, specifically medicine.
  • 1882-1885: Studied in Spain (Madrid), specializing in ophthalmology, and traveled to France and Germany.
  • 1887: Published Noli Me Tangere in Berlin, a novel critical of the Spanish colonial regime.
  • 1887-1891: Continued writing, traveling throughout Europe (including Spain, France, Germany, and Austria), and engaging in reformist activities.
  • 1891: Published El Filibusterismo, his second novel, also critical of Spanish rule.

Return to the Philippines and Execution

  • 1892: Returned to the Philippines, founded the Liga Filipina, and was subsequently exiled to Dapitan, Mindanao.
  • 1892-1896: Practiced medicine, continued his artistic and scientific pursuits, and established a school in Dapitan.
  • 1896: Accused of rebellion and sedition, arrested, and imprisoned in Fort Santiago.
  • December 30, 1896: Executed by firing squad in Bagumbayan (now Luneta), solidifying his status as a national hero.
